
 Task 1: Employee Performance Ranking with Compensation Context
Requirement: 
Write a query that shows for each sale:
- The employee's full name and department
- The sale date and the running total of sales revenue (Quantity * UnitPrice * (1 - Discount)) for that employee, ordered by sale date
- The average sale amount for that employee's department (as a separate column, not partitioned by employee)
- A CASE statement that classifies each sale as:
  - 'Above Target' if the sale amount is greater than the department's average
  - 'At Target' if equal to the department's average
  - 'Below Target' if less than the department's average
- The employee's RANK based on their total sales revenue (all their sales combined) within their department
- Use LAG to show the previous sale amount for that employee

HINT: Calculate revenue as: `(s.Quantity * p.UnitPrice * (1 - s.Discount))`

 Task 2: Product Category Analysis with FIRST_VALUE and LAST_VALUE
Requirement:
Write a query that shows for each product:
- Product name and category
- Total quantity sold (for that product)
- Total revenue generated (for that product)
- FIRST_VALUE of the product name (ordered by total revenue descending) within each category - this shows the best-selling product in that category
- LAST_VALUE of the product name (ordered by total revenue descending) within each category - this shows the worst-selling product in that category
- The difference between the product's revenue and the category's maximum revenue (use a window aggregate for this, not a subquery)
- A CASE that labels the product as 'Category Leader', 'Middle Performer', or 'Category Trailing' based on whether it's the first, somewhere in between, or last in the category ranking

NOTE: Since you need to order by total revenue, you'll need to aggregate first, then apply window functions. Remember to use `ROWS BETWEEN UNBOUNDED PRECEDING AND UNBOUNDED FOLLOWING` for LAST_VALUE to work correctly.

 Task 3: Monthly Sales Trend with NTILE and LEAD
Requirement:
Write a query that shows monthly sales performance:
- The month (as a name, e.g., 'January') and year
- Total monthly sales revenue
- NTILE(4) to divide months into quartiles based on total monthly revenue (highest revenue = 1st quartile)
- LEAD of the next month's total revenue (to see the upcoming month's performance)
- The percentage change from the current month to the next month (use the LEAD value you calculated)
- A CASE statement that labels each month as:
  - 'Excellent' if in the top quartile (NTILE = 1)
  - 'Good' if in the 2nd quartile
  - 'Average' if in the 3rd quartile
  - 'Poor' if in the bottom quartile
- Also show the running total of monthly revenue (cumulative sum ordered by month)

HINT: You'll need to group by month/year first, then apply window functions. Use `DATENAME(MONTH, SaleDate)` and `DATEPART(YEAR, SaleDate)`.

 Task 4 (Bonus - Most Challenging): Combined Insights Dashboard
Requirement:
Write a single query that combines all your skills to create a comprehensive view. Show:
- Department
- Employee name
- Total sales revenue for that employee
- The employee's RANK and DENSE_RANK within their department (based on total revenue)
- The difference between this employee's revenue and the department's highest earner (use a window function)
- A CASE statement that assigns a 'Performance Tier':
  - 'Executive' if the employee is in the top 2 of their department
  - 'Senior' if they are in positions 3-4
  - 'Junior' if they are in positions 5+
- The total sales revenue for the department (as a separate column)
- The employee's contribution percentage (employee revenue / department revenue * 100)
- Use FIRST_VALUE to show the highest-earning employee's name in each department as a separate column
- Order the results by department, then by revenue descending

IMPORTANT: This will require careful thinking about how to get the department total and the highest earner's name using only window functions (no subqueries/CTEs).

---

 Important Reminders:
1. No subqueries or CTEs - use only the tools you've learned
2. All window functions must be in the SELECT clause (not in WHERE)
3. Pay careful attention to `PARTITION BY` and `ORDER BY` clauses
4. For `LAST_VALUE`, remember to specify the frame
5. For percentage calculations, handle NULLs appropriately (though you shouldn't have any here)
